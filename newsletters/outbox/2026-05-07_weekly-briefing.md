**EuroOilWatch Weekly**
**Thursday, 7 May 2026**

Welcome to this edition of EuroOilWatch Weekly.

The European picture remains under strain. EuroOilWatch's dashboard, updated on **6 May 2026 at 07:31**, shows **107 days of petrol cover**, **99 days of diesel cover**, and **94 days of jet fuel cover** at EU level. It also shows **20 of 27 EU countries** hold critical reserves in at least one fuel type. The same dashboard shows **Brent at $108.29/barrel (€99.63)**, **EU average petrol at €1.777/litre**, **EU average diesel at €1.913/litre**, and Dutch front-month **TTF gas at €43.74/MWh**, with EU gas storage **34.1% full**. (EuroOilWatch)

**This week's takeaway:** markets are reacting to peace-talk headlines, but Europe's real fuel-security problem has not been solved. Reuters reported that Brent rebounded to **$101.81/barrel** by **06:15 GMT** on **7 May** after Wednesday's sharp sell-off, while a separate Reuters report said the global oil supply shock is still likely to worsen even if the conflict ends soon because inventories and emergency reserves have been drawn down and Gulf shipments would still take weeks to reach refiners. (Reuters)

## Three numbers that matter

**EU average diesel — €1.913/litre.** Diesel remains the clearest pressure point for freight, logistics, agriculture, and industrial users across the bloc. Eleven countries are diesel-critical on the EuroOilWatch dashboard, including major economies such as **Germany** and **Spain**. (EuroOilWatch)

**Jet fuel cover — 94 days.** Jet fuel is still Europe's broadest vulnerability. Eighteen of 27 EU countries are at critical or warning levels for jet fuel, including major aviation hubs such as **Germany, France, and the Netherlands**. (EuroOilWatch)

**Active German supply disruption — live since 1 May.** EuroOilWatch flags an active disruption from **1 May 2026**: Russia has halted **Kazakh crude supply via Druzhba to Germany**, leaving the **PCK Schwedt refinery** with roughly **17% less crude**. Germany says national fuel security is not in jeopardy, but the site notes the risk of **regional pricing effects**. (EuroOilWatch)

## What is happening now

One of the clearest warning signs is in aviation fuel. Reuters reported on **6 May** that **Israel will transfer jet fuel to Germany** after Berlin requested assistance following the Strait of Hormuz crisis. Germany said it does not currently face a physical shortage, but the fact that Berlin is seeking external jet-fuel support shows how live the pressure remains going into the summer travel season. (Reuters)

Europe's problem is also being worsened by what is happening outside Europe. Reuters reported on **7 May** that Asia's exports of refined fuels have plunged as Hormuz remains effectively closed: **jet fuel exports fell to 596,000 bpd in April from 1.54 million bpd before the war**, while **diesel exports fell to 2.22 million bpd from 3.54 million bpd**. That does not all flow to Europe, but it tightens the global market Europe must buy into. (Reuters)

There is also a broader policy shift underway. Reuters reported on **5 May** that EU energy ministers will discuss **domestic gas production** next week as governments try to limit the economic damage from the Iran war. Reuters said the EU still relies on imports for about **80% of its gas needs**, which helps explain why gas security is moving back up the political agenda alongside oil and refined fuels. (Reuters)

The page also shows EU and per-country gas storage from AGSI/GIE: EU aggregate **34.1% full** going into the refill season, with the gap to the regulatory **90% target by 1 November** at **55.9 percentage points**. ## What to watch next

- Whether Brent stays around **$100+** even if diplomacy advances, because the physical recovery in supply will lag far behind the headlines. (Reuters)
- Whether Europe's jet-fuel problem starts easing or spreads further, despite emergency support such as the Israel-to-Germany transfer. (Reuters)
- Whether the **Schwedt/Druzhba** disruption begins to show up more visibly in regional pricing and product flows across North-West Europe. (EuroOilWatch)
- Whether Brussels turns next week's emergency discussion into a wider rethink on gas supply, storage, and domestic production before next winter. (Reuters)
