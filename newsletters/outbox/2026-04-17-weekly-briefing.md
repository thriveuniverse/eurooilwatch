## This Week

The EU fuel security picture deteriorated this week with **19 of 27** member states now holding critical stock levels in at least one fuel category. **Romania** faces the most acute shortage with diesel reserves at just **20.2 days**, while jet fuel stocks across the bloc average only **91.1 days** — barely above the 90-day minimum threshold.

## Live Risk Signals

**Tropical Cyclone SINLAKU-26** has reached Category 1 strength affecting the **Northern Mariana Islands and Guam**, though this poses no direct threat to European supply routes. No significant seismic activity or refinery thermal anomalies detected in oil-relevant regions over the past week.

## EU Reserve Status

Current reserves stand at **98.7 days petrol**, **97.4 days diesel**, and **91.1 days jet fuel**. While headline numbers appear adequate, the jet fuel situation is precarious with **17 countries** below safe levels. For logistics operators, this means potential supply constraints and elevated pricing for aviation fuel across multiple markets. Major economies including **Germany, France, Italy, and Spain** all show critical levels in multiple fuel types, indicating widespread vulnerability rather than isolated regional issues.

## Market Prices

**Brent crude** trades at **$98.34 per barrel**, down **1.06%** but remaining elevated due to ongoing Middle East supply disruptions. EU average prices sit at **€1.784/L petrol** and **€2.021/L diesel**, with dramatic regional variations — a **€0.976 spread** between Netherlands and Malta petrol prices highlighting internal market stress.
