Welcome to this edition of EuroOilWatch Weekly.

The continental picture is still under real strain. EuroOilWatch's dashboard today shows **EU average reserves at 102 days for petrol, 96 days for diesel, and 90 days for jet fuel**, while **22 of 27 EU countries are below the 90-day benchmark for at least one fuel type**. The same dashboard shows **Brent at $112.18/barrel (€103.21/barrel)**, **EU average petrol at €1.777/litre**, and **EU average diesel at €1.913/litre**. ([EuroOilWatch](https://eurooilwatch.com/))

**This week's takeaway:** Europe is not facing a simple oil-price spike. It is facing a wider fuel-security squeeze. Brent has continued to trade volatile through 30 April, holding above $109 and reaching session highs above $114 on intraday moves, as the market reacts to the risk of a prolonged Middle East disruption with the **Strait of Hormuz still closed**. ([Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/business/energy/oil-prices-extend-gains-us-iran-war-deadlock-keeps-supply-off-market-2026-04-30/))

## Why Europe is still exposed

The European Central Bank held its deposit rate at **2%** on Thursday and warned that the Iran war is pushing up energy-led inflation while weighing on euro-area growth. The ECB said the longer the war continues and the longer energy prices remain high, the stronger the likely impact on broader inflation and the economy. ([Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/business/finance/ecb-keeps-rates-hold-warns-about-iran-war-hit-2026-04-30/))

That pressure is already showing up in national data. Reuters reported that **France's harmonised inflation rate rose to 2.5% in April**, above forecasts, driven by energy prices, while **Germany's EU-harmonised inflation accelerated to 2.9%** in April, with energy prices up **10.1% year on year**. ([Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/markets/us/french-preliminary-inflation-25-april-above-forecasts-energy-costs-bite-2026-04-30/))

## Jet fuel is becoming Europe's sharpest weak spot

Reuters reported on 28 April that **no jet fuel loaded from the Middle East is headed for Europe in April**, the first such month in Kpler data since 2017. OECD Europe consumes about **1.6 million barrels per day** of jet fuel and kerosene but produces only about **1.1 million bpd**, leaving a shortfall of at least **500,000 bpd** that imports must fill. Reuters also reported European jet fuel prices back above **$200/barrel** and cited an IEA warning that Europe could start seeing physical shortages by **June** if only half of normal Middle East supplies are replaced. ([Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/business/energy/europes-jet-fuel-imports-middle-east-stop-raising-supply-crunch-fears-2026-04-28/))

That is no longer just a theoretical risk for airlines. Air France-KLM said today that it expects its annual fuel bill to rise by **$2.4 billion** to **$9.3 billion**, that supply looks stable only until **June**, and that it is hoping for EU guidance and **Jet-A imports from North America** to help offset the loss of Middle East barrels. ([Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/business/air-france-klm-revises-capacity-outlook-geopolitical-uncertainty-2026-04-30/))

## Germany and 1 May — another layer of regional friction

Tomorrow brings another Europe-specific complication. Russia is set to stop **Kazakh crude flows via Druzhba to Germany** from **1 May**, affecting the **PCK Schwedt refinery**, which supplies most of Berlin's fuel and had relied on Kazakhstan for about **17%** of its supply. Germany says petroleum-product security is not in jeopardy, and Brandenburg said on 29 April that **up to 80%** of Schwedt's crude supply is secured for May while officials work on alternatives including **Gdansk**. Even so, this is another sign that Europe is adding regional supply friction at exactly the wrong time. ([Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/business/energy/russia-stop-kazakh-oil-flows-german-refinery-via-druzhba-berlin-says-2026-04-22/))
