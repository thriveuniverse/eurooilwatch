## Urgent Supply Alert — 18 April 2026

The Strait of Hormuz has been reclosed by Iran as of today, reversing the brief diplomatic opening announced yesterday (17 April). This is a significant development requiring immediate attention from logistics operators and procurement teams.

---

## What Happened

Iran declared the Strait open on 17 April as part of a ceasefire agreement. Within hours, Tehran reversed course — citing the United States maintaining a naval blockade on Iranian ports as a violation of the deal terms. The IRGC has stated Hormuz will remain under "strict military management" until the US restores freedom of navigation for Iranian vessels.

**Key numbers:**
- Tanker transits: ~19/day (vs normal 138/day) — a **90% reduction**
- Loaded tankers queued in the Gulf: **230+**
- US blockade: deterred at least 13 merchant ships from transiting on day one

---

## Both Gulf Corridors Now Simultaneously Closed

This matters because the Strait of Hormuz closure does not stand alone. The Red Sea / Bab-el-Mandeb route has been effectively closed to commercial traffic since late 2023 due to Houthi attacks. With Hormuz now also closed, **both primary oil export routes from the Gulf are simultaneously blocked** — an unprecedented compound disruption.

For EU buyers, all Middle Eastern crude and products must now route via the Cape of Good Hope — or not at all.
